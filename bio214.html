Gourmet Chocolate For Sweet Lovers

Posted on November 26, 2012 by nhvwc1ma

Perhaps when it comes to a chocolate lover’s passion for chocolate, nothing beats the taste and grandeur that only belongs to Belgian chocolate .

Belgian chocolate is considered as one of the finest delicacies and the highest quality of chocolates because of its taste and some claim it as sinful indulgence, not too sweet and not too bitter, just that – perfect.

Where Belgian Chocolate Comes From

Belgian chocolates are made from the purest cacao beans.

The beans are checked for their quality and those that do not pass the stringent quality are rejected.

The beans will then be processed until the beans achieve a smooth and creamy consistency.

The chocolatiers do not add preservatives to their chocolate, which is probably one of the reasons why the chocolates are expensive, at the same time taste natural, and would not last long unlike other chocolates.

Belgian Chocolate Types

There are two types of Belgian chocolate : praline and truffles.

Belgian pralines, also called as Belgian bonbons, are Belgian chocolates filled with any of fruits, wine, cream and nut paste.

The first praline was introduced by Belgian chocolatier, Jean Neuhaus and since then is considered as one of the best manufacturers of Belgian chocolate.

Belgian truffles, on the other hand are made with truffles, a luxurious and one of the best delicacies in the world.

The truffles are made of a combination of chocolate, cream and ganache.

Truffles can also be infused with alcohol flavourings such as wine, brandy, rum and vodka.

One can also find truffles with almond paste fillings which are the most common type of nut filling.

Buying Belgian Chocolates

As buying pure Belgian chocolates is quite expensive, it is important that one be assured that one is purchasing real and authentic Belgian chocolate.

There are hundreds of stores selling Belgian chocolates but if you want the satisfaction or the guarantee then you can try checking out gourmet shops, as more often than not they would have your favourite Belgian chocolate brand.

Another way of buying your chocolate is through online sites.

Many well-known brands now have internet sites and shipping which makes it possible for one to purchase their favourite brand.

Ordering online is also ideal as Belgian chocolates only have limited shelf life and prolonged exposure can reduce its distinctive taste and quality.

Belgian chocolates can only last 21 days if not refrigerated, so it is important that you only take out what you can consume to preserve the flavour of the chocolates and prolong its shelf life.
