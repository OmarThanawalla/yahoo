Volume 79, Issue 4

On the cover: Unveiling the asynchronous activation of sodium and potassium channels. In this issue, Lacroix et al. (pages 651â€“657) identified the molecular determinants responsible for the sequential activation of voltage-gated sodium (Nav) and potassium (Kv) channels, a fundamental requirement for the generation and propagation of nerve impulses. By manipulating these determinants, they created a slower Nav channel and a faster Kv channel, which are illustrated here as the famous paradox of the Aesop's fable wherein the tortoise, representing the fast Kv channel, wins a race against the hare, representing the slow Nav channel.

2013 Anuradha Rao Memorial Travel Award

This award of $1000 will be given annually to a graduate student or post-doc to cover expenses for travel to the Society for Neuroscience meeting. The award will be presented at the 2013 SfN meeting in San Diego.

To apply, please submit a short essay (500 words or fewer) describing your scientific background and career interests, and what you hope to gain from attending the meeting. Applications can be sent to neuron@cell.com, and the deadline is Sept. 10.

This award represents the efforts of Anuradha's family to remember a brilliant young scientist with a thirst for knowledge and to pay their respects to the science so dear to her.

CURRENT ISSUE
Featured Article | Scene Processing in the Macaque Temporal Lobe
Previous studies have shown that areas of the human brain respond selectively to scenes. Using fMRI, microstimulation, and electrophysiology, Kornblith et al. functionally characterize a network for scene processing in the macaque. PREVIEW. Preview by Epstein & Julian.  FREE
Featured Review | The Sensory Neurons of Touch
Low-threshold mechanoreceptors (LTMRs) are critical for touch perception. Here, Abraira & Ginty review LTMR structure and function, emphasizing their unique tuning properties and the organizational logic of their axonal projections, which underlie our capacity for perceiving the richness of the tactile world.  FREE
Featured Perspective | The Basal Ganglia and Decision Making
Perceptual decisions are complex processes that engage numerous, interacting brain regions. Ding and Gold review recent work that has begun to identify specific roles of the subcortical basal ganglia network in helping to form, act on, and evaluate these decisions. FREE
