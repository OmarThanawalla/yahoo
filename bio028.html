NASA Human Research Program (HRP)

NASA's Human Research Program (HRP) conducts research and develops technologies that allow humans to travel safely and productively in space. The Program uses evidence from data collected on astronauts, as well as other supporting studies. These data are stored in the research data repository, Life Sciences Data Archive (LSDA).

Space and Clinical Operations

The Space and Clinical Operations Division mission is to optimize the health, fitness, and well being of flight crews, their dependents and the employees of the Johnson Space Center in Texas, and the White Sands Test Facility in New Mexico. Astronaut medical data collected for clinical purposes are archived, maintained, and analyzed by the Lifetime Surveillance of Astronaut Health (LSAH).
