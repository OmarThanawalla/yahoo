A new nova in the sky

 

There’s a new “nova” in the sky. Not a supernova, just a nova. But, what is a nova?

A classical nova happens in a special kind of tightly-orbiting binary star system: one where a relatively normal star pours a stream of hydrogen onto the surface of a companion white dwarf.
