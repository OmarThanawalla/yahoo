News

  • Peroxisomes and photomorphogenesis

    Posted on Jul. 22, 2013

    This work analyzed microarray data to reveal the up- and down-regulation of subsets of Arabidopsis peroxisomal genes by light, and provided a summary of current knowledge of the role of light in peroxisome proliferation and plant photomorphogenesis at the molecular level.

     

  • Transorganellar complementation redefines the biochemical continuity of ER and chloroplasts

    Posted on Jul. 22, 2013

    We demonstrate that the presence of unique contact sites between the ER and chloroplast allows for the free exchange of non-polar plastid metabolites between these organelles. These results provide a new insight into our understanding of the integration and regulation of organelle-spanning pathways that synthesize non-polar metabolites in plants.

  • A bHLH-Type Transcription Factor JAM1 Negatively Regulates Jasmonate Signaling in Arabidopsis

    Posted on Jul. 16, 2013

    This work identifies the bHLH transcription factor, JAM1, as a novel negative regulator of jasmonate responses. JAM1 represses the transcription of the target genes of MYC2 by competing for binding to cis-acting elements.

     

  • A chemical complementation approach reveals genes and interactions of flavonoids with other pathways

    Posted on Jul. 16, 2013

    This study provides genetic and biochemical evidence that Arabidopsis can sense flavonoids as a signal for modulating responses to biotic and abiotic stress conditions. 

     

  • Altered lipid composition and nutritional value of Arabidopsis leaves by an algal acyltransferase

    Posted on Jul. 16, 2013

    Enhancement of acyl-CoA-dependent triacylglycerol (TAG) synthesis in vegetative tissues is widely discussed as a potential avenue to increase the energy density of crops. Here, we report the identification and characterization of Chlamydomonas reinhardtii diacylglycerol acyltransferase type two (DGTT) enzymes and use DGTT2 to alter acyl carbon partitioning in plant vegetative tissues. 

     

  • Dynamics of PEN3 focal accumulation during Pseudomonas syringae infection of Arabidopsis

    Posted on Jul. 16, 2013

    PEN3, an Arabidopsis ABC transporter protein, is required for full bacterial resistance of Arabidopsis and has a dynamic localization pattern in response to Pseudomonas syringae infection.
