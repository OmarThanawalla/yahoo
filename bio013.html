White Blood Cells

White blood cells are blood components that protect the body from infectious agents. Also called leukocytes, these cells play an important role in the immune system by identifying, destroying, and removing pathogens, damaged cells, cancerous cells, and foreign matter from the body. The image above depicts a white blood cell called a neutrophil (purple) ingesting MRSA bacteria (yellow). Image Credit: NIAID

Video Games Can Increase Cognitive Function

Saturday August 24, 2013

Credit: Franky242 / FreeDigitalPhotos.net

Can playing certain video games improve cognitive flexibility? Queen Mary University of London and University College London researchers have conducted studies that suggest that playing real-time strategy games improves a person's problem solving abilities. Playing these type of games increases an individual's ability to adapt to situations and to think about multiple concepts at a given time.

According to the researcher Dr. Brian Glass, "Previous research has demonstrated that action video games, such as Halo, can speed up decision making but the current work finds that real-time strategy games can promote our ability to think on the fly and learn from past mistakes." The researchers state that understanding how playing these games improves cognitive flexibility could lead to the development of treatments for individuals with certain brain injuries or attention deficit disorders.

Bacteria Induce Pain

Friday August 23, 2013

Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) Bacteria
Credit: CDC/ Janice Haney Carr/ Jeff Hageman, M.H.S.

Researchers have discovered that bacteria are responsible for the pain that accompanies skin infections. While studying Staphylococcus aureus infections, it was discovered that the bacteria induce pain by secreting substances that communicate with the host's sensory neurons. Once activated, the neurons also suppress the immune system response at the site of the infection.

According to the study's author, Isaac Chiu, "If we could block pain in infected tissues and also block what pain neurons do to the immune system, it could help us treat bacterial infections better. We found that major parts of the immune system are not necessary for pain during infection, but that bacteria themselves are the source of much of the pain." It is not known why neurons suppress the immune system in these types of infections. It has been postulated that this action may prevent further damage to tissue that could result from an immune system inflammatory response.

Virus-Derived Particles Kill Leukemia Cells

Friday August 16, 2013

Researchers are developing a new method for treating blood cancer. Virus-derived particles have been developed that destroy blood cancer cells, while leaving normal cells unharmed. Ottawa researchers altered viruses so that the particles could no longer replicate. The viruses however, maintained the ability to kill human leukemia cells in lab tests.

According to researcher Dr. David Conrad, "We were delighted to see that this novel therapy was very safe at high doses, and worked extremely well in our laboratory leukemia models. We hope to test this in patients in the near future." The researchers state that this type of therapy is promising due to its long-lasting effect on leukemia, along with its relatively minor side effects.

Chocolate Improves Brain Health

Saturday August 10, 2013

Credit: FreeDigitalPhotos.net

An American Academy of Neurology study has revealed that drinking hot chocolate can improve brain health. Drinking two cups of hot cocoa a day was shown to improve memory and thinking skills in the elderly. It also improved blood flow to the brain for individuals with impaired blood flow issues.

According to the study's author, Farzaneh A. Sorond, "As different areas of the brain need more energy to complete their tasks, they also need greater blood flow. This relationship, called neurovascular coupling, may play an important role in diseases such as Alzheimer's." In the study, the researchers used two types of hot chocolate. One type of hot cocoa was rich in the antioxidant flavanol, and the other was poor in flavanol. The results showed no differences between the two types of hot chocolate.
