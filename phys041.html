About UFFC

Ultrasonics, Ferroelectrics and Frequency Control is a Society of the IEEE, with an international membership of approximately 2200 technologists. Our technology is diverse today, with our common historical origins all traceable to the phenomenon of piezoelectricity.

Our field of interest includes theory, technology, materials, and applications relating to:

  • The generation, transmission, and detection of ultrasonic waves and related phenomena.
  • Medical ultrasound and associated technologies.
  • Ferroelectric, piezoelectric, and piezomagnetic materials.
  • Frequency generation and control, timing, and time coordination and distribution.

These technologies enable sophisticated medical diagnostics, improved sensors for industrial control, lead free electronic components, synchronization for telephony and data transport, and precision timing technology for Global Navigation Satellite Systems.

The UFFC is organized into three groups, as our name implies. We typically hold three symposia annually with an objective of strong collaborative interchange and a special focus on the students who study our technology. Some of the best minds in our community volunteer to teach at tutorials and short courses at these symposia.

EIC-Elect Steven Freear will assume the position of EIC for T-UFFC effective February 1. Marj has served as EIC since January 1, 2008, her dedication and selfless devotion to the T-UFFC significantly... read more contributing to the publication in many ways. Prior to becoming EIC, she assisted with the transition from manual paper processing to Manuscript Central. As EIC, Marj has enhanced publication timeliness and overseen growth of the Transactions from approximately 2500 to about 3000 pages per year. She has also implemented systematic plagiarism checking, along with numerous metrics that are useful in monitoring performance of Associate Editors, publication timeliness, and other useful factors. Finally, Marj prepared for and presented the most recent T-UFFC Society Review, which received exemplary ratings from IEEE and was cited to other Societies as including "best practices" for Society Transactions. We thank Marj for a job well done, and wish Steven the best in his new role as EIC. (posted 1 February 2013)

The Distinguished Lecturer Program is an important feature of UFFC. A Distinguished Lecturer is chosen at each Fall AdCom meeting... read more to serve an 18-month period beginning on July 1 of the following yearThe Distinguished Lecturer is selected not only because of personal stature and contribution to a field, but also because of broad knowledge of the field. Each lecturer presents between 20 and 40 lectures all over the world at institutions and places of interest to her/him and her/his research. Groups desiring to invite a lecturer should either contact the lecturer directly or write to Bernhard Tittmann, Vice-Chair of the Awards Committee.
