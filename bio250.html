Current issue

Review

Metalloproteinases and their natural inhibitors in inflammation and immunity

Rama Khokha, Aditya Murthy & Ashley Weiss

Metalloproteinases are the unsung modifiers of immune responses. Among other activities, they regulate several key signalling pathways, modify the availability of chemokines and influence innate and adaptive immune cell function. As the authors highlight, a lack of these crucial enzymes has dramatic immunological consequences.

Current issue

Perspective

Broadly neutralizing antibodies and the search for an HIV-1 vaccine: the end of the beginning

Peter D. Kwong, John R. Mascola & Gary J. Nabel

In this Opinion article, the authors provide an overview of the recent work that has been carried out investigating broadly neutralizing HIV-1 antibodies and their thoughts on the future prospects of the antibody-based prevention of HIV-1 infection.

Current issue

Review

Mitogen-activated protein kinases in innate immunity

J. Simon C. Arthur & Steven C. Ley

This Review summarizes our current understanding of the regulation and function of mitogen-activated protein kinases (MAPKs) in innate immunity, as well as the mechanisms by which pathogens manipulate MAPK activation and the potential of targeting MAPK pathways for the treatment of inflammatory diseases.

Current issue

Review

Scavenger receptors in homeostasis and immunity

Johnathan Canton, Dante Neculai & Sergio Grinstein

This article describes the immunological functions of the scavenger receptors. The authors focus on the structural properties that enable these receptors to respond to a diverse range of both self and non-self ligands. They also discuss the contribution of scavenger receptors to inflammatory diseases.

Current issue

Review

The cup runneth over: lessons from the ever-expanding pool of primary immunodeficiency diseases

Joshua D. Milner & Steven M. Holland

This Review describes the recent insights that have been made from studying primary immunodeficiency diseases (PIDs). Notably, patients with gene mutations that lead to PIDs often show very different disease phenotypes from those predicted from studies of gene-deficient mice. Thus, these individuals are an invaluable resource for increasing our understanding of human immunology.

Noticeboard

Crossword for the Day of Immunology 2013

Monday 29th April is the international Day of Immunology 2013. To celebrate, the Nature Reviews Immunology team has prepared a cryptic immunology crossword for our readers. Click on the link above to access a PDF version of the crossword — enjoy!

Focus on The inbetweeners: innate-like lymphocytes

The recent identification of several subsets of innate lymphoid cells (ILCs) and the evolving understanding of the function of other innate-like lymphocytes, including γδ T cells, NKT cells and marginal zone B cells, suggest that there are no clear boundaries between innate and adaptive immunity. This Focus features articles describing these recent advances and discussing their implications for the future of immunological research.

Nature Immunology animation: Immunology in the gut mucosa

The gut mucosa is the body's largest immune compartment. It's home to a vast population of microflora as well as a key site of pathogen entry. Nature Immunology in collaboration with Arkitek Studios have produced an animation unravelling the complexities of mucosal immunology in health and disease. View the free animation. Produced with support from Janssen Pharmaceutical Companies of Johnson & Johnson. Animation by Arkitek studios.

Poster on The immune response to HIV

This Poster summarizes how HIV establishes infection at mucosal surfaces, the ensuing immune response to the virus involving DCs, B cells and T cells, and how HIV subverts this response to establish a chronic infection. The Poster is freely available thanks to support from STEMCELL Technologies.

Focus on Transplant immunology

Transplantation has therapeutic applications not only for patients experiencing organ failure, but also in other clinical settings, such as cancer and autoimmunity. The specially commissioned Review articles in this Focus issue describe the recent developments that have been made in the field of transplant immunology. Importantly, the authors discuss the promise of new immunotherapies that prevent transplant rejection and graft-versus-host disease without inducing broad immunosuppression.

Focus on Tumour immunology & immunotherapy

This joint Focus issue from Nature Reviews Cancer and Nature Reviews Immunology explains how key immune cells function in the tumour microenvironment and discusses the potential of immunotherapy to treat cancer. Access is free until the end of September 2012, thanks to support from Boehringer Ingelheim.

Focus on Monocytes and macrophages

Macrophages, first described at the end of the 19th century by Élie Metchnikoff, have a central role in maintaining tissue homeostasis but also provide signals for the activation of adaptive immune cells. The specially commissioned review articles in this Focus discuss our current understanding of the homeostatic and pathogenic functions – as well as the development, recruitment and transcriptional regulation – of monocytes and macrophages.

10th anniversary crossword

To celebrate the 10th anniversary of Nature Reviews Immunology, we have 'cross presented' some words for you in this immunology-themed puzzle. Click on the above link to access a PDF of the crossword — the answers can be found on the second page of the PDF.

Poster on Dendritic cells: controllers of adaptive immunity

This Poster provides an overview of the key functions of dendritic cells in initiating protective immunity to pathogens and in promoting tolerogenic immune responses that contribute to tissue homeostasis. The Poster is freely available thanks to support from BioLegend.

Podcast: 60 years of Immunology at the MRC National Institute of Medical Research (NIMR), Mill Hill, UK

Since the start of immunological research at NIMR in 1950, scientists at the institute have been involved in some key discoveries. Key figures at the Institute — past and present — recall their time at the institute in this joint podcast from Nature Immunology and Nature Reviews Immunology in collaboration with NIMR.

Focus on Metabolism and immunology

Obesity-linked metabolic diseases, such as type 2 diabetes and cardiovascular disease, are associated with a state of low-grade inflammation. This Focus features articles that explore the interplay between metabolism and the immune system, explaining how inflammation drives type 2 diabetes, how fat-tissue-derived mediators are involved in inflammation and metabolic disease, and how T cell metabolism is linked to T cell fate. The Focus is free to registered users, thanks to support from sanofi-aventis.

Poster on NK cells: receptors and functions

This Poster provides an overview of the key receptors that regulate the activity of natural killer (NK) cells in humans and how NK cells protect against abnormal cells and modulate adaptive immune responses. The Poster is freely available thanks to support from STEMCELL Technologies.

Focus on Therapeutic antibodies

This Focus issue brings together specially commissioned articles on the basic biology of antibody receptors and on the important advances and further prospects for the development of more efficient, affordable and safer therapeutic antibodies. Produced with support from MedImmune.

Poster on T cells: the usual subsets

T cells can be grouped into different subsets based on their effector functions and molecular profile. This Poster summarizes our current understanding of the main T cell subsets that have been described and provides an overview of their various roles in host immunity. The poster is freely available thanks to support from Abcam

Poster on Myeloid-derived suppressor cells

This poster provides an overview of the development, activation and effects of myeloid-derived suppressor cells (MDSCs). An understanding of the immunosuppressive and tissue repair functions of MDSCs will be crucial for effective therapeutic targeting. The poster is freely available thanks to support from BioLegend

Poster on Proximal tyrosine kinases that initiate T cell activation

This poster provides an overview of these mechanisms for several proximal tyrosine kinases that have a prominent role in TCR signalling and highlights how small molecule inhibitors could mechanistically block their activity to modulate T cell-mediated immune responses. The Poster is freely available thanks to support from Epinova DPU and Cellzome.

Poster on regulatory T cells

This Poster provides an updated overview of the development, phenotype and functions of regulatory T cells, in particular those subsets that express the transcription factor forkhead box P3 (FOXP3). The Poster is freely available thanks to support from StemCell Technologies.
