Selected resources

  • EthxWeb — a richly indexed database that consolidates interdisciplinary bioethics material covering 1974-2009. Explore more databases prepared by the BRL.
  • PubMed — comprises over 21 million citations for biomedical literature from MEDLINE, life science journals, and online books. View recently added documents in core bioethics journals or limit to free full text documents.
  • Philosopher's Index — the primary index to the literature of philosophy. Available online if your library has purchased a subscription to the database.
  • PhilPapers — a comprehensive directory of online philosophical articles and books by academic philosophers.
  • EthicShare — a community website to research, share, collaborate, and participate in the field of ethics.
  • BELIT (Bioethics Literature Database) — provides access to approximately 470,000 records from integrated German, American and French databases.
  • Google Scholar — provides a search of scholarly literature across many disciplines and sources.
For a list of all academic databases available at Georgetown University view the A to Z Database List.

Featured projects & collections

Acadia Institute Interview Collection
Renée C. Fox, a sociologist, and Judith P. Swazey, a historian, became a 'team of two' in this ethnological study of the new field of bioethics, that began with the foundation of the Hastings Center in 1969, and two years later, the Kennedy Institute of Ethics. Their book, 'Observing Bioethics', was based on a series of interviews Fox and Swazey did with a number of well-known bioethicists.

By arrangement with LeRoy Walters, then Director of the Kennedy Institute of Ethics, transcripts of the interviews were archived at the Bioethics Research Library. We are grateful to the participants, who authorized the BRL to make the interviews accessible through this DigitalCollectoin to those interested in the history of bioethics.
