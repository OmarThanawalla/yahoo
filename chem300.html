Some Big Changes this Summer

Years ago I featured a "Molecule of the Month" on this website. Originally the molecules were presented with a Chime plugin, which was then replaced by JMol, a Java applet. Because of Java's problematic security issues and the fact that Java applets don't run in the increasingly important smart phone and tablet world, I've been on the lookout for a way to bring my Molecule Library into the 21st century. Well, I just stumbled on jsMOl, the successor to JMol. I've updated the Molecule Library so that the 3d-molecules are presented in HTML 5, with the option of using the original JMol applet if you so choose. While I was re-doing the molecules, I also brought all of the molecules to a new menu system and provided access to some off-site databases as well.

On Friday, June 27 as I made the transition to a new server. I've upgraded my account at BlueHost to a provide faster, more reliable performance. While I have no complaints at all about the service I have been getting from them, it was obvious that during peak hours the access to site resources was sometimes slow. I am certain that the upgrades in place will provide better service and faster response times. With the help of the amazing folks at BlueHost, the move was seemless and quite fast.

As more and more of us move to primarily mobile access to the Internet, the limitations of Java and Flash technology become important considerations. For years I have used a free product from iSpring to post my PowerPoint presentations in Flash format. This summer I've ponied up the money to buy iSpring Pro 7. It is allowing me to post the PowerPoint files in HTML 5 format, which is compatible with recent desktop browsers, but more importantly, mobile browsers as well. Of course, I'm still making them available in Flash format and the .ppsx slide show format. Both the HTML 5 and Flash presentations have some great new features, such as a built-in pen, highlighter and eraser. They also contain a "Resources" tab that provides quick access to my review activities on the subject being studied. I have completed the Biology PowerPoints, Chemistry PowerPoints, and AP Chemistry PowerPoints.

For those of you with iPads, the HTML 5 presentations provide the opportunity to download iSpring's FREE iPad app, which allows you to save the HTML5 PowerPoint presentation to your iPad for offline use.

Now that I'm done with the PowerPoint updates, I'm also going to invest time in the AP Chemistry review activities. AP Chemistry is working under a new framework in 2013 - 2014, and from the practice test that the College Board released to AP teachers, it is apparent that the test questions will be much more dependent on graphics, such as tables, charts, graphs and diagrams. During the last half of the summer, and through the next school year, I will be re-writing many of the AP Chemistry review activities (and my own class assessments as well) to better align with the new question format.

The Chemistry Page will give first year chemistry students access to all of the notes, worksheets, labs, Powerpoints and other important documents for my General Chemistry classes. AP Chemistry

I am teaching a section of AP Chemistry in the 2013 - 2014 school year. The AP Chemistry Page hosts the resources for the class, which include PowerPoints in HTML 5 and Flash format, notes, review activities, and a calendar. I am committing a lot of time and energy toward converting these resources to align with the College Board's new AP Chemistry Framework. Though I developed the resources for my AP students, they will probably also prove useful to college students taking a first year chemistry course.
